The Solo Realtor

Sellers Only Agent

I do not represent buyers. I do not split my loyalty. I represent homeowners and sellers only—with a sharper, cleaner way to sell.

What “Sellers Only” Means

“Sellers only” means I never enter into an agency relationship to represent a buyer. It does not mean refusing to show homes to or accept offers from unrepresented buyers. When I show my own listing to an unrepresented buyer, I am acting on behalf of the seller—the buyer is a customer, not my client. My duty stays with you. I have not represented a buyer since 2021 and will not do it again.

Undivided Loyalty

No split attention. No internal tug-of-war. You are the only client in the transaction.

Cleaner Confidentiality

Your timing, motivation, and negotiating posture stay aligned with the seller mission only.

Stronger Negotiation

Every recommendation is made through the lens of what benefits you, not what makes the deal easier for everyone else.

Clearer Brand

Sellers know exactly what I stand for. Referrals become easier because the positioning is easy to describe and hard to forget.

Why Dual Agency Is the Conversation Most Sellers Should Be Having

Dual agency asks the homeowner to accept diluted loyalty. The seller often trusts the personality of the agent rather than the structure of the relationship. I remove the conflict by removing the second client relationship. Unrepresented buyers can still view the property and submit offers—but they are not my clients. I represent the seller side only.

If you want someone fully focused on protecting your side, that person should not also be representing the side negotiating against you. That is not anti-buyer. That is pro-clarity.
