Why Interest Rates Matter More Than Ever

Mortgage interest rates are one of the most significant factors in real estate. They directly impact home affordability, buyer demand, and overall market activity. As we move through 2025, Santa Clarita’s real estate market is being shaped by interest rate trends that will determine whether prices rise, fall, or stabilize.

How Rising Interest Rates Have Affected Santa Clarita Real Estate

Over the past few years, we’ve seen significant fluctuations in interest rates. In 2021 and 2022, mortgage rates were at historic lows, fueling one of the hottest real estate markets in history. However, as inflation concerns grew, the Federal Reserve raised rates aggressively in 2023 and 2024.

What Should Buyers Do Right Now?

If you’re a buyer, you may be wondering: Should I wait for interest rates to drop? While waiting may seem like a good idea, it could actually cost you more in the long run. Here’s why:

1. Home Prices Will Likely Rise

As soon as rates drop, more buyers will flood the market. Increased demand will drive home prices higher, meaning the savings from a lower interest rate could be wiped out by a higher purchase price.

2. Less Competition Right Now

With higher rates, fewer buyers are in the market. That means you face less competition, giving you more negotiating power. You may be able to secure a home below asking price or with seller concessions.

3. You Can Refinance Later

If you buy now and rates drop in the future, you always have the option to refinance to a lower rate. This allows you to secure a great home today while still benefiting from future rate cuts.

What Should Sellers Do Right Now?

If you’re a seller, you might be wondering: Should I wait for rates to drop? Here’s why selling now can be a great strategy:

1. Inventory Remains Limited

Even though rates are high, there still isn’t a surplus of homes on the market. That means sellers can still command strong prices if they position their homes correctly.

2. Motivated Buyers Are Still Looking

Many buyers in today’s market are highly motivated—some are relocating, others are looking for investment properties, and many just want to lock in a home now before prices rise again.

3. You Can Offer Incentives

Sellers who offer interest rate buy-downs or closing cost credits can attract more buyers and make their homes more affordable without reducing the price.

Final Thoughts: Act Before the Market Shifts

The Santa Clarita real estate market is in a transitional phase. While higher interest rates have slowed demand, we are likely heading into a period where rates will drop, competition will increase, and home prices will rise again.

Buyers who wait may end up paying more for the same home, while sellers who list now can still take advantage of limited inventory and serious buyers.
