The Problem: We’re Grazing Like Cattle

Let’s cut the crap. Most of us are eating all damn day. A chip here, a snack there, maybe a “small” 2,000-calorie lunch because you “deserve it.” Sound familiar? I’ve been there. At 365 pounds, I was a walking heart attack, tying my shoes like it was an Olympic event. The reality? Constant eating keeps your insulin spiked, your body storing fat, and your pants size creeping up. You’re not even hungry half the time—it’s just habit. Autopilot munching.

The result? Freshman 20 turns into Middle-Age 60. You’re buying bigger clothes every few years, wondering why you’re out of breath. Maybe you’ve heard about those weight loss drugs everyone’s hyping—Ozempic, Wegovy, whatever. They sound like a miracle, right? Inject some magic, watch the pounds melt. But hold up. Those drugs come with risks, like muscle loss or unknown long-term effects. And at 550 pounds, like my buddy Cat, the real question isn’t the needle—it’s whether the drug’s worth the gamble.

Fasting, on the other hand? It’s free, natural, and worked for me. Dropped 135 pounds in 7.5 months at 52. No injections, no BS. Just results. Let’s dive into why fasting kicks ass, how it stacks up against drugs, and how you can start—whether you’re 200 or 600 pounds.
