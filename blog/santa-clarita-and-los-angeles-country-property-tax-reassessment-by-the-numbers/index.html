What Is Property Tax Reassessment?

Property tax reassessment occurs when a county assessor re-evaluates the value of a property to determine its new tax base. In California, property taxes are governed by Proposition 13, passed in 1978, which limits annual property tax increases to no more than 2% unless specific events trigger a reassessment. When a property is reassessed, its taxable value is updated to reflect its current market value, which can significantly increase or decrease the tax bill.

In Santa Clarita, reassessments are handled by the Los Angeles County Assessor’s Office. The process is critical because it directly affects how much you’ll pay in property taxes as a homeowner or what buyers will face after purchasing your home.

Proposition 13: The Foundation of California Property Taxes

To understand reassessment, you need to know the basics of Proposition 13. This landmark legislation caps property taxes at 1% of the property’s assessed value at the time of purchase, plus a maximum 2% annual increase for inflation. This means if you bought a home in Santa Clarita for $500,000 in 2010, your initial tax bill would be around $5,000 per year, increasing by up to 2% annually, even if the home’s market value skyrockets.

However, certain events can trigger a reassessment, resetting the tax base to the current market value. For example, if that same home is now worth $900,000 and is sold, the new owner’s tax bill could jump to $9,000 annually (plus the 2% annual increase). Understanding these triggers is crucial for both buyers and sellers.

Common Questions About Property Tax Reassessment

How Often Are Properties Reassessed in Santa Clarita?

Properties are only reassessed upon a change of ownership, new construction, or other qualifying events. Otherwise, taxes increase by up to 2% annually under Proposition 13.

Can I Appeal a Reassessment?

Yes, if you believe the assessed value is too high, you can file an appeal with the Los Angeles County Assessor’s Office. I can guide you through this process—contact me at https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/blog/contact.
