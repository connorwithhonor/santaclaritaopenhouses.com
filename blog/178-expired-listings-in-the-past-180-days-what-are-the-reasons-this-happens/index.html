Why Do Listings Expire? The Top Culprits

1. Pricing: The Cardinal Sin of Real Estate

At the end of the day, it all boils down to price. If a home is priced too high, it’s like trying to sell a beat-up cruiser for the price of a Tesla. Buyers aren’t stupid—they know value when they see it. Those 178 expired listings? Most likely, their asking prices were out of sync with what the market was willing to pay.

2. Poor Marketing: Your Agent’s Gotta Hustle

Not all agents are created equal. I’ve seen listings with photos taken on a flip phone from 2005, blurry and dark as a dive bar at midnight. If your agent isn’t showcasing your home like it’s the star of a Hollywood blockbuster, you’re in trouble.

3. Property Condition: Fix It or Price It

Buyers aren’t just looking at price—they’re eyeballing the competition. If your home is the odd one out in the neighborhood (think dated fixtures, shag carpet from the ‘70s, or a layout that screams “what were they thinking?”), it’s going to struggle.

4. Market Dynamics: Buyers Are Getting Choosier

The Santa Clarita market isn’t the Wild West it was a few years ago. With 776 active listings, buyers have options, and they’re not afraid to walk away from overpriced or underwhelming homes.
