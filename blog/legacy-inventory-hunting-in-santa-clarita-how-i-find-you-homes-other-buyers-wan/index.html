Executive Summary (TL;DR)

Most buyers chase only what’s on the MLS today. I add a second lane: legacy inventory—properties that were canceled, withdrawn, or expired. Those sellers previously tried to sell and didn’t succeed. Some still want to move, but they’re not relisted. With a precise buyer brief from you, I proactively contact those owners—yes, knocking on doors, letters, calls, and neighbor-intel—to uncover opportunities the public never sees. This approach works best when we’re crystal-clear on criteria, prepared to move fast, and honest about tradeoffs.

Your Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is door-knocking rude? A: Not when it’s done professionally, with a specific buyer match and a respectful opt-out. My approach is concise, credentialed, and deferential.

Q: Why would a seller who failed once want to try again? A: Because now it’s one qualified buyer, not a parade. Lower disruption, clearer terms, and we can often price the prior friction into a better structure.

Q: Will I overpay because it’s off-market? A: Not on my watch. We comp rigorously and structure offers to protect you. Off-market doesn’t mean over market—it often means right market with better terms.

Q: How fast can this move? A: If the fit is strong and your file is prepped, we can move from hello to agreement quickly. The bottleneck is rarely the buyer—it’s clarity and coordination. That’s my job.

Q: What if the property has issues (permits, solar, Mello-Roos, HOA drama)? A: Then we do what pros do: diligence, documentation, and price/term calibration. I’ve handled all of the above many times.

Q: What if the seller wants to test the market again publicly? A: Then we make a real offer worth considering now—or we wish them well and keep hunting. No chasing. We maintain leverage with preparation, not pressure.
