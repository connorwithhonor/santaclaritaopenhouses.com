Understanding Bankruptcy Sales: Opportunities and Pitfalls

One intriguing aspect of the current market is the presence of bankruptcy sales, which Connor highlighted in his June 27 update. These properties can seem like incredible deals, but they come with complexities that require careful navigation.

What Are Bankruptcy Sales?

Bankruptcy sales occur when a homeowner files for bankruptcy, and their property is listed for sale to settle debts. These homes are often priced significantly below market value—sometimes $600,000 to $700,000 less than comparable properties. For example, a home worth $1.2 million might be listed at $500,000 to attract buyers quickly.

Why They’re Priced Below Market Value

The low listing price is a strategy to generate interest and spark bidding. However, as Connor explains, the final sale price is subject to court approval. The bankruptcy court, along with the mortgage lender and other creditors, must agree on the sale terms. This means the home rarely sells for the initial list price and often goes for closer to its fair market value.

The Court Approval Process

The court approval process can take weeks or months, depending on the complexity of the bankruptcy case. Buyers need to be patient and prepared for potential delays. Additionally, the property may not be available for in-person viewings until the court approves, which can complicate the decision-making process. Connor advises against making offers on bankruptcy properties without seeing them in person unless you’re an experienced investor comfortable with the risks.
