While the “alien intelligence” metaphor is evocative, it’s important to ground our understanding in reality. No discussion about AI is complete without addressing one of its most controversial aspects: job displacement. The specter of AI-induced unemployment looms large in many debates, with the promise of technological utopia often shadowed by the fear of widespread economic disruption.

It’s a concern that has real-world implications. As AI systems become more adept at performing tasks that were once the exclusive domain of humans, many worry that entire industries could be upended. However, it’s essential to take a balanced view. Yes, AI will displace certain jobs—but it will also create new opportunities. Historical precedent shows us that technological revolutions, while disruptive in the short term, eventually lead to the emergence of new industries and roles that we cannot yet fully imagine. Think of the digital revolution: while it rendered some jobs obsolete, it also gave birth to entirely new sectors such as digital marketing, cybersecurity, and data analytics.
