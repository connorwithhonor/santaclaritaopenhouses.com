The Bigger Picture: California’s Real Estate Rollercoaster in September 2025

First off, California’s not the golden child it used to be in real estate lore. Remember the pandemic frenzy where homes sold faster than toilet paper flew off shelves? Those days are dust. As of early September 2025, listings statewide have taken a nosedive—down from last month and even year-over-year. Inventory’s tighter than a Gen X budget after Y2K paranoia. Experts are calling it a shift to “neutral” territory, where sellers aren’t calling all the shots anymore. In Northern California, buyers are flexing a bit more muscle; homes are hanging around longer, giving you time to actually think before bidding wars erupt.

Down south? Similar story, but with a twist. San Francisco’s market has clawed back to pre-pandemic pricing—yeah, that “normal” everyone’s chasing. Oakland might follow suit. Overall, home prices are dipping in some pockets (think 0.9% drop forecasted for 2025 by some pros) while inching up in others. No one’s predicting a crash—demand’s still solid, thanks to steady jobs and folks fleeing pricier states—but expect flatter growth over the next five years. Mortgage rates? They’re the wildcard. Fifteen-year loans could slide to around 5.5% by late 2025, making that dream SCV pad more doable if you’re not lugging massive debt.

SCV Market Snapshot: The Numbers Don’t Lie (But Sellers Might Wish They Did)

Alright, pulling from the freshest data (last 7 days ending September 21, 2025), SCV’s residential scene—covering condos, townhomes, and single-family homes (attached and detached) across Acton, Agua Dulce, Castaic, Canyon Country, Newhall, Saugus, Stevenson Ranch, Val Verde, and Valencia—is a mixed bag of opportunity and caution. Total properties in play? 1,133 across all statuses. That’s not a flood, but it’s enough to keep things interesting. Average list price sits at $893,724, with homes averaging 2,135 sqft and a cheeky $436.11 per square foot. Days on market? 64 on average—longer than a bad blind date, signaling buyers have breathing room.
