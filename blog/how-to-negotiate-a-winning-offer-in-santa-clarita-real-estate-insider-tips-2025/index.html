The Biggest Mistake Buyers Make: Verbal Negotiations

In the excitement of house hunting, it’s tempting to ask your realtor to “feel out” the seller’s agent. Maybe you want to know if the seller would take less than the list price or what terms they’re open to. But as Connor MacIvor warns, these verbal discussions between agents—often called “ex parte” talks—can do more harm than good.

Real-World Example

Imagine you’re eyeing a $900,000 home in Valencia’s Northbridge community. Your agent calls the seller’s agent and asks, “Would they take $850,000?” The seller’s agent, without consulting their client, says, “Probably not, but they’re motivated.” You get excited, submit a low offer, and the seller rejects it outright, feeling insulted. Had you submitted a written offer with data justifying the $850,000 (e.g., recent sales of similar homes), the seller might have countered, sparking a productive negotiation.

How to Craft a Data-Driven Offer

The key to a winning offer is preparation. In Santa Clarita’s competitive market, sellers expect offers that reflect the property’s value and market conditions. Arbitrary lowballs—say, offering $800,000 on a $900,000 home without justification—rarely succeed. Instead, Connor recommends building a case with hard data. Here’s a step-by-step guide to crafting a data-driven offer.

Step 4: Justify Your Offer

When submitting an offer below list price, include a cover letter or notes explaining your reasoning. For example:

“We’re offering $825,000 based on recent sales of comparable 2,000-square-foot homes in Saugus, averaging $830,000. The property requires $30,000 in updates (kitchen, flooring), which we’ve factored into our offer. We’re pre-approved and flexible on closing dates.”

Common Negotiation Scenarios in Santa Clarita

Every home purchase is unique, but certain scenarios pop up frequently in Santa Clarita. Here’s how to handle them with Connor’s guidance.

Scenario 1: Low Offer on a Fixer-Upper

You find a $950,000 home in Canyon Country needing $60,000 in repairs. You want to offer $875,000. Strategy: Submit a written offer with comps of similar homes selling for $900,000–$920,000, plus a repair estimate. Highlight your flexibility (e.g., quick close). Connor will present the offer professionally, increasing your chances of a counter.

Scenario 2: Multiple-Offer Situation

A $800,000 condo in Valencia gets three offers. Strategy: Offer slightly above list (e.g., $810,000) if comps support it, and include an escalation clause (e.g., “We’ll beat any offer by $2,000 up to $820,000”). Waive minor contingencies if you’re pre-approved. Connor’s negotiation skills shine here, ensuring your offer stands out.

Scenario 3: Motivated Seller

A Saugus home has been on the market for 60 days, suggesting seller motivation. Strategy: Offer 5–10% below list, backed by comps and market data showing longer days on market. Request seller concessions (e.g., closing costs). Connor will gauge the seller’s priorities to tailor your offer.

Scenario 4: New Construction

You’re eyeing a new build in Castaic. Strategy: Research builder incentives (e.g., upgrades, rate buydowns) and compare to resales. Offer close to list, as builders rarely budge, but negotiate extras. Explore new construction listings with Connor’s help.
