Understanding the Santa Clarita Real Estate Market in June 2025

A Slowing Market: Days on Market Doubled

The Santa Clarita real estate market is showing signs of a slowdown as of June 2025. One of the most significant indicators is the doubling of days on market (DOM). Properties that once took about 20 days to enter escrow are now averaging 40–50 days, depending on location, price, and buyer interest. This shift doesn’t signal a full-blown buyer’s market, but it does mean sellers need to work harder to stand out, and buyers have more leverage to negotiate.

According to recent data, Santa Clarita currently has approximately 775 active listings, a slight decrease from the previous day’s 800. This includes 98 new listings and 11 properties marked as “coming soon.” While inventory is robust, the pace of sales has slowed, reflecting cautious buyer behavior and increased scrutiny during appraisals. For a detailed breakdown, check out the Santa Clarita Market Report.

Inventory Trends and Buyer Drive

The increase in inventory—nearing record highs not seen in over two years—offers buyers more choices, particularly in neighborhoods like Valencia and Canyon Country. However, buyer drive remains a critical factor. Properties that are well-priced and staged effectively still attract multiple offers, while overpriced homes linger on the market. Sellers must adapt to this reality by focusing on presentation and pricing, which we’ll explore in the next sections.

For buyers, the increased inventory means opportunities to find homes in desirable areas, such as gated communities or homes with pools. A pristine front yard, fresh paint, and a welcoming entryway signal that your home is well-maintained and worth the asking price. Temperature Control: Creating a Comfortable Environment

Beyond aesthetics, the sensory experience of your home matters. In Santa Clarita’s warm June climate, a cool interior can be a game-changer. Set your thermostat to 69–70 degrees to provide relief from the summer heat, making buyers feel instantly comfortable. In colder months, a warm, cozy interior (around 72 degrees) showcases your home’s heating system and creates an inviting atmosphere.

This strategy isn’t just about comfort—it’s about psychology. A buyer who feels relaxed and at ease is more likely to linger, imagine themselves living in the space, and submit an offer. Pricing Your Home Right in a Competitive Market

The Danger of Overpricing

In a market where homes are taking longer to sell, pricing your home correctly is paramount. Overpricing is one of the biggest mistakes sellers make, leading to extended DOM and reduced buyer interest. When a home sits on the market too long, buyers may assume something is wrong with it, even if it’s in excellent condition.

To avoid this pitfall, work with a realtor who conducts a thorough comparative market analysis (CMA). A CMA compares your home to similar properties that have recently sold in your neighborhood, accounting for factors like square footage, upgrades, and location. Connor MacIvor specializes in creating data-driven CMAs to ensure your home is priced competitively. Request a free market analysis to get started.

The Role of Appraisals

Even if you price your home correctly, appraisals can impact the sale. Lenders require appraisals to confirm that the home’s value aligns with the loan amount. If an appraiser determines the value is lower than the offer price, the buyer may need to cover the difference in cash or renegotiate. This is where a skilled realtor like Connor comes in, providing appraisers with detailed data on recent sales and upgrades to justify the price.

For example, Connor recently assisted a seller in Saugus whose home was initially undervalued by an out-of-town appraiser. By submitting a comprehensive CMA and highlighting local market nuances, Connor successfully increased the appraised value, saving the sale. Mortgage Rates and Federal Reserve Impacts

Federal Reserve Rate Cuts: A Trickle-Down Effect

The Federal Reserve’s recent actions are shaping the Santa Clarita real estate market. In late 2024, the Fed reduced the federal funds rate to a range of 4.75–5.00%, sparking optimism about lower mortgage rates. While the fed funds rate doesn’t directly dictate mortgage rates, it has a trickle-down effect, influencing the 10-year Treasury note and, subsequently, 30-year fixed mortgage rates.

As of June 2025, mortgage rates are hovering near a “magic number” that could spur refinancing and homebuying activity. Some economists predict rates could dip into the low 5% range by late 2025, making homeownership more affordable. For the latest insights, visit our mortgage rates page.

Opportunities for Buyers and Refinancers

Lower mortgage rates create opportunities for both buyers and existing homeowners. For buyers, a 1–2% rate reduction can save thousands over the life of a loan. For example, on a $1 million mortgage, a drop from 7% to 5% could reduce monthly payments by hundreds of dollars. Homeowners with higher-rate loans from 2022–2023 are also exploring refinancing options to unlock equity or lower payments.

However, competition may increase as rates fall, driving up demand for homes in Santa Clarita. Buyers should get pre-approved to strengthen their offers—learn how at getting pre-approved.

The “Golden Handcuffs” Phenomenon

The transcript mentions “golden handcuffs,” referring to homeowners reluctant to sell due to low mortgage rates from years past (e.g., 3–4%). As rates approach the low 5% range, these homeowners may feel more comfortable listing their homes, potentially increasing inventory further. This could benefit buyers but challenge sellers to differentiate their properties. Stay informed with our newsletter for real-time market updates.

Buyer Strategies: Why You Need a Diligent Agent

Navigating Santa Clarita’s Tract-Based Market

Santa Clarita’s real estate market is unique due to its tract-based structure, where homes within the same neighborhood (or “tract”) share similar designs and values. This makes it critical to compare properties within the same tract when crafting offers. An inexperienced or out-of-town agent may overlook these nuances, leading to overpriced offers or missed opportunities.
