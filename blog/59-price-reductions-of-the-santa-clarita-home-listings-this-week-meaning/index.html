Understanding Contingencies and Negotiation Strategies

1. The Key Contingencies in a Home Purchase

Contingencies are conditions in the contract that protect the buyer and allow you to back out if certain criteria aren’t met. The most common contingencies include:

A. Inspection Contingency

Gives you time to review the home inspection report and request repairs or credits. If major issues arise, you can cancel the contract without penalty.

B. Appraisal Contingency

Ensures the home’s value aligns with the lender’s appraisal. If the appraisal comes in lower than the purchase price, you can renegotiate or back out.

Check out current Santa Clarita Home Prices to compare appraisal values.

C. Financing Contingency

Allows you to cancel if you’re unable to secure financing. This is why getting pre-approved (or better yet, fully underwritten) is crucial before making an offer. Need help with financing? Check out Santa Clarita Mortgage Rates.

D. Title and HOA Contingencies

Protects you from issues like undisclosed liens, property boundary disputes, or unexpected homeowners’ association (HOA) fees.
