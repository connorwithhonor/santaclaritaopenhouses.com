What Has Changed in Mortgage Lending in 2025?

The real estate market is always evolving, and 2025 is no exception. Buyers today have access to a wider range of mortgage products designed to meet different financial situations, and lenders are adjusting their requirements to accommodate changing economic conditions.

More Loan Options for Buyers

One of the biggest shifts in the mortgage industry is the increase in loan programs available to buyers. Unlike previous years, where conventional financing was the primary route for most borrowers, 2025 brings a broader set of options, including:

The expansion of these loan programs means that more buyers can qualify for a home, even those with non-traditional income sources or less-than-perfect credit.

Buyer Mistakes That Can Derail a Home Loan

Despite the availability of various financing options, buyers often make critical mistakes that can lead to loan denials or costly delays. Tim Slominsky shared some of the most common errors homebuyers make when applying for a mortgage.

Making Large Purchases Before Closing

Many buyers assume that once they have been pre-approved for a loan, they are free to make major purchases. However, buying a new car, financing furniture, or increasing credit card debt before closing can significantly impact a buyer’s debt-to-income (DTI) ratio.

Lenders calculate DTI to ensure that a borrower can afford their monthly mortgage payments in addition to other financial obligations. Any significant increase in monthly debt payments could result in loan disqualification or force the buyer into a less favorable loan program.

Depositing Large Amounts of Cash Into Bank Accounts

Another mistake buyers make is depositing large sums of cash into their bank accounts without a clear paper trail. Mortgage lenders require documented sources of income, and cash deposits that cannot be sourced can create red flags in the approval process.

Buyers should keep all deposits trackable by using direct bank transfers, payroll deposits, or documented sales transactions. If a buyer receives financial gifts from family members to assist with a down payment, they must follow the lender’s guidelines for documenting gift funds.

Changing Jobs or Employment Status

Lenders verify employment status multiple times during the mortgage process, including just before closing. If a buyer quits their job, switches to self-employment, or takes a pay cut, their mortgage approval could be jeopardized.

Buyers should maintain consistent employment throughout the home buying process and discuss any planned career changes with their lender before making any moves.

Why Local Lenders Matter in Santa Clarita

One of the most critical decisions a buyer makes is choosing the right lender. In today’s digital world, many buyers are tempted by large online mortgage companies advertising low rates. However, working with a local lender like Tim Slominsky provides key advantages that can make a substantial difference in both the home buying experience and financial outcomes.

Faster Loan Approvals and Closings

Local lenders offer quicker loan processing times compared to national mortgage companies, where applications often pass through multiple departments and outsourced underwriting teams. Tim noted that he has successfully closed loans in as little as 12 to 15 days for well-qualified borrowers, significantly faster than the 30 to 45-day timelines of many large institutions.

Direct Communication and Personal Service

Unlike online lenders, who may only be reachable during standard business hours, a local lender provides direct access and personalized service throughout the loan process.

Mortgage Loan Options for 2025 – Finding the Right Fit for You

By Connor MacIvor – Santa Clarita’s Top Realtor with 26+ Years of Experience

Watch the Full Interview with Tim Slominsky

Introduction

Choosing the right mortgage loan is one of the most important steps in the homebuying process. With the real estate market in Santa Clarita evolving, buyers need to understand the loan programs available in 2025 and how each one fits their specific financial situation.

In my latest interview with Tim Slominsky, a lender with over 45 years of experience, we discussed the most popular home loan programs, who qualifies for them, and how they can help buyers achieve homeownership.

In this post, we will cover:

Top Mortgage Loan Programs for 2025

The mortgage industry is constantly changing, but in 2025, lenders are offering more flexible options to help buyers qualify for home loans. Below are some of the best loan programs available today.

1. FHA Loans

Best for: First-time homebuyers, buyers with lower credit scores, and those with limited savings.

The Federal Housing Administration (FHA) loan is one of the most popular options for homebuyers, particularly for those who need a lower down payment.

FHA loans are an excellent choice for buyers looking to purchase a home with lower upfront costs.

2. VA Loans (For Veterans & Active-Duty Military)

Best for: Military service members, veterans, and eligible surviving spouses.

VA loans are one of the best mortgage programs available, offering 100% financing with no down payment required.

For veterans and active-duty military looking to buy a home in Santa Clarita, the VA loan is often the best option available.

3. USDA Loans (100% Financing for Rural and Suburban Areas)

Best for: Buyers looking to purchase in eligible rural and suburban areas, including parts of Santa Clarita.

USDA loans provide 100% financing, meaning no down payment is required.

Areas such as Acton, Agua Dulce, and parts of Canyon Country may qualify for USDA financing.

4. Conventional Loans

Best for: Buyers with strong credit and a larger down payment.

Conventional loans are not backed by the government and require higher credit scores but offer competitive interest rates.

Buyers who can put down 20% or more avoid mortgage insurance costs, making conventional loans attractive for those with strong financials.

5. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) Loans for Investors

Best for: Real estate investors looking to purchase rental properties.

DSCR loans are unique because they qualify borrowers based on the income potential of the property, rather than their personal income.

For buyers looking to invest in Santa Clarita rental properties, DSCR loans provide a flexible option that allows them to scale their real estate holdings.

6. Bank Statement Loans (For Self-Employed Borrowers)

Best for: Self-employed individuals, business owners, and freelancers.

Traditional mortgage loans require tax returns, which can be difficult for self-employed buyers who write off many expenses. Bank statement loans allow buyers to qualify based on their bank deposits instead of tax returns.

Why Pre-Approval is Essential in 2025

With increased competition in the Santa Clarita real estate market, pre-approval is more important than ever. A simple pre-qualification is no longer enough.

Pre-Approval vs. Pre-Qualification

In competitive situations, sellers prefer buyers with full loan pre-approval, as it shows financial strength and shortens closing timelines.

Next Steps

Understanding your mortgage loan options is the first step toward homeownership. By choosing the right financing program and securing a full pre-approval, you can make competitive offers and increase your chances of securing the best home loan.

Introduction

California wildfires have always posed challenges for homeowners and buyers, but in 2025, these challenges have become even more significant. How Wildfires Affect Mortgage Lending

When a wildfire occurs near a home that is under contract, the lender will typically require a reinspection by an appraiser to ensure the property is still standing and has not sustained fire damage. This can delay closing or even cause the loan to be denied if the home is deemed unsafe.

Additionally, some lenders are now restricting loans in high-risk fire zones because insurance coverage is increasingly difficult to obtain. If a buyer cannot secure homeowners insurance, they cannot close on the home, as lenders require an active policy for financing approval.

Steps Buyers Must Take to Secure Insurance

Buyers looking to purchase homes in wildfire-prone areas must take proactive steps to secure insurance and ensure their loan closes on time.

1. Start Shopping for Insurance Early

Buyers should begin contacting insurance providers as soon as they are in escrow, rather than waiting until the final days before closing. If coverage is difficult to obtain, this allows time to explore alternative options.

2. Work with an Insurance Broker

Insurance brokers have access to multiple carriers and can help buyers find coverage when traditional providers decline.

How Sellers Can Prepare for Insurance-Related Hurdles

Home sellers in fire-prone areas should anticipate potential insurance-related concerns from buyers and take steps to make their property more insurable.

2. Provide a Recent Fire Inspection Report

3. Address Fire Risks Before Listing

Clearing brush, installing fire-resistant materials, and ensuring easy access for emergency vehicles can improve a home’s insurability.
