The Science Behind Fat Loss vs. Weight Loss

It’s crucial to understand that when we talk about weight loss, we often lump together several elements—fat, muscle, water, and even bone density. However, the real enemy in our health battle is fat, not necessarily weight. In a memorable segment of my talk (see the srt transcript timestamps for context), I explain:

“When you go on a caloric restricted diet, your metabolism actually goes down. It’s not just about cutting calories—it’s about preserving muscle mass and burning fat efficiently.”

Studies have shown that while a severe caloric deficit may produce rapid results on the scale, it can be detrimental in the long run. The body, when deprived of adequate nutrition, shifts into a conservation mode—slowing the metabolism and even sacrificing muscle tissue to sustain life. This is why fasting protocols that allow the body to tap into its stored fat (rather than a constant caloric restriction) can be more beneficial.

The benefits of fasting are multi-fold. Not only does it shift your metabolism from burning sugar to burning fat, but it also triggers autophagy—a process by which the body clears out damaged cells and renews itself. This process is crucial for overall health, longevity, and even mental clarity.

In my own words from the Fat To Fit manuscript:

“I torched off my fat by letting my body access its own stored energy. It’s not about starving—it’s about recalibrating your body to become a fat-burning machine.”

In this light, fasting emerges as a powerful tool to not only reduce body fat but also to revitalize overall health. And as we will explore in later sections, how you reintroduce food—your refeed protocol—plays an equally critical role.

Understanding Fasting Protocols and Their Benefits

Fasting is a practice that has been misinterpreted by many. Some equate it to starvation; however, the deliberate act of fasting, when done under proper guidance, offers an array of benefits that go far beyond simple weight loss.

Fasting vs. Caloric Restriction

When you restrict calories for an extended period, your body adapts by lowering your metabolic rate. This adaptation often leads to plateaus in weight loss and can make subsequent dieting even more challenging. On the other hand, fasting temporarily suspends regular eating patterns—giving your body a break from processing food continuously. This pause allows your metabolism to switch gears and focus on burning stored fat.

Consider the following excerpt from my discussion:

“Fasting is not about deprivation; it’s about tapping into the body’s natural energy reserves. When you fast, you’re essentially rebooting your metabolism, allowing it to function more efficiently.”

In my personal journey, I’ve experimented with extended fasts—up to 21 days of a Daniel fast—and learned that while the initial days may seem challenging, the body quickly adapts. Hunger hormones, like ghrelin, recede, and you enter a state of ketosis where fat is the primary fuel source.

As I explain in my manuscript:

“Once you’re in ketosis, the hunger fades and your body begins to use its stored fat for fuel. It’s a profound transformation that goes beyond the physical—it’s mental, emotional, and spiritual.”

Refeed Protocol: Perhaps the most critical aspect of any fasting regimen is the refeed period. Breaking your fast must be done gently and mindfully. A hasty reintroduction of calories, especially from processed foods, can undo all your hard work. I learned this lesson the hard way—after a long fast, I once indulged in a massive pizza and ice cream meal, only to feel the consequences soon after.

Instead, honor your body with a gradual refeed: start with bone broth, progress to eggs or light proteins, and slowly reintroduce more complex carbohydrates and healthy fats.

The key takeaway here is to view fasting not as a punishment, but as an opportunity—a chance to listen to your body and reset its internal equilibrium.

The Role of Hormones: Testosterone and Beyond

One of the most eye-opening revelations in my journey was the impact of hormones on fat loss. As we age, our testosterone levels naturally decline, affecting muscle mass, energy, and overall metabolism. Low testosterone can accelerate fat accumulation while diminishing muscle tissue—the very component we want to preserve.

In my early days, I noticed that despite working out and watching my calorie intake, I wasn’t getting the results I craved. It wasn’t until I addressed my hormone balance—specifically, testosterone—that I saw a dramatic improvement. As noted in my Fat To Fit book:

“Testosterone therapy, when needed and properly monitored, can be a game changer. It’s not for the young, but for those in their later years, it helps maintain muscle and accelerates fat loss.”

Hormonal balance is not only about physical appearance—it affects mood, cognitive function, and even your risk of age-related diseases. With proper medical guidance, hormone therapy can restore energy levels and make your fasting regimen even more effective.

For anyone considering this route, it is imperative to consult with a healthcare provider, get comprehensive blood work, and discuss both the benefits and potential risks. The goal is to achieve a natural balance that supports a healthier, more energetic lifestyle.

The number was far lower than I had ever imagined—but more importantly, I felt lighter, not just physically, but emotionally. That was the turning point: realizing that fat loss was not merely about aesthetics, but about reclaiming control over my life.

Over time, I refined my strategies. I learned the importance of mixing fasting protocols with resistance training. I discovered that even moderate exercise—like walking, calf raises, and light weightlifting—was essential to maintain muscle mass. Every workout was a step toward not only losing fat but also regaining a sense of self-worth.

One key lesson from my personal battle with food addiction is that every day is an opportunity to rewrite your story. Even on the days when I felt defeated, I picked myself up, examined my choices, and moved forward. It’s a message I want to share with every reader: the journey is continuous, and every small victory counts.

Tapping into Your Inner Strength: Practical Tips and Daily Habits

Transformation isn’t only about diet and exercise—it’s about cultivating habits that empower you every day. Here are some practical tips that I have relied on throughout my journey:

1. Start Your Day with Intention

Before the chaos of daily life takes over, take a few minutes to center yourself. Whether it’s through meditation, journaling, or simply setting a clear intention for the day, this practice grounds you and primes you for success.

2. Embrace Regular Movement

Exercise doesn’t have to be grueling. Simple activities—like a daily walk or a series of resistance exercises—help maintain muscle and support your metabolism. I always remind myself: “Walk. Lift. Move.” Even when the scale isn’t budging, the health benefits accumulate over time.

3. Monitor Your Hunger Signals

Learning to listen to your body is crucial. During a fast, the hunger hormone ghrelin may send strong signals, but these often come in waves. Over time, your body learns to differentiate between true hunger and habitual eating. When you notice these waves, have a glass of water, sip some black coffee, or simply sit with the feeling until it passes.

4. Honor Your Refeed

Breaking a fast requires mindfulness. Start small—opt for nutrient-dense foods like bone broth, eggs, or a light salad. Gradually increase your food intake and avoid the temptation to overindulge. Remember, the refeed is not an excuse to binge; it’s a carefully orchestrated part of your overall plan.

5. Keep a Journal

Documenting your progress not only holds you accountable but also lets you reflect on the highs and lows of your journey. I have countless entries where I dissect my feelings about food, my body image, and the triumphs over self-sabotage. Over time, this journal becomes a powerful tool for self-transformation.

As I once wrote in my manuscript, “I started writing journals when I switched from being a full-time LAPD officer to a reserve officer. Every entry was a step toward understanding and overcoming my relationship with food.” That daily practice of self-honesty is as important as any physical workout.

Practical Fasting Tips and Protocols

Fasting, especially extended fasts, requires preparation, care, and respect for your body. Below is a detailed protocol inspired by my personal experience and refined through years of trial and error:

Before the Fast

Preparation is Key: The night before starting your fast, take a long, warm shower. Trim your nails, take a moment for self-care, and set a positive mindset. Your body will soon be entering a state of recalibration.

During the Fast

Stay Hydrated and Monitor Electrolytes: If you’re doing a water fast, ensure that you drink enough water. For those who choose a dry fast, consult your doctor and monitor your well-being closely. In my experience, a combination of water, black coffee, and herbal teas (without any sweeteners) keeps hunger at bay.

Mind the Hunger Waves: Hunger tends to come in pulses. When it hits, practice deep breathing or take a short walk. Often, the hunger subsides after 15–45 minutes.

Breaking the Fast

Honor Your Refeed: The first meal after an extended fast should be gentle on your digestive system. Begin with clear liquids like bone broth, then slowly introduce easy-to-digest proteins such as eggs or a small salad. Avoid jumping straight to heavy, calorie-dense foods.

A wise piece of advice from my manuscript encapsulates this perfectly:

“Honor the Re-Feed. Your first meal after fasting is not just breakfast—it’s the meal that breaks your fast. Treat it with reverence, and your body will thank you.”

Remember, every fasting protocol should be tailored to your body’s needs. What works for one person might not work for another, so always keep an open dialogue with your healthcare provider.

Integrating Exercise: Building Muscle While Losing Fat

While fasting can help you burn fat, maintaining muscle is equally important—especially as you age. I’ve always maintained that exercise is the counterbalance to the metabolic slowdown that comes from caloric restriction. Even on fasting days, a few sets of resistance training (calf raises, squats, dumbbell rows, and bicep curls) help to preserve muscle tissue.

The research supports this: When testosterone levels are balanced and the body is in a fasting state, the muscle-sparing effects are remarkable. A balanced regimen of weight training and moderate cardio, such as brisk walking, can make all the difference. In my Fat To Fit book, I write:

“Work out every day—whether it’s heavy lifting or simply walking. Every movement counts, and every step brings you closer to the body you deserve.”

Incorporate a mix of strength training, HIIT, and low-intensity cardio. Experiment with different modalities until you find what feels right for your body.
