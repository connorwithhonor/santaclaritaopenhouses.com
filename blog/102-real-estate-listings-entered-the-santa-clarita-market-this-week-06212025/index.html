A Buyer’s Market on the Horizon? Analyzing the Numbers

The flood of new listings is more than just a welcome sight for buyers; it’s a key indicator of a market in transition. For the past few years, Santa Clarita has been a staunch seller’s market, characterized by low inventory and high competition. While we are not officially in a buyer’s market yet, the scales are undeniably tipping in that direction. Let’s look at the evidence.

Days on Market (DOM) is Climbing

One of the clearest signs of a shifting market is the “Days on Market” (DOM) metric, which measures how long a home takes to sell. While many of this week’s listings are brand new (DOM of 0-7 days), the overall trend in Santa Clarita has been a steady increase in the time it takes for a property to go under contract. Homes that would have been snapped up in a single weekend are now seeing a sales cycle of several weeks.

This is fantastic news for buyers. It means you have time to breathe, to think, and to perform your due diligence. You can schedule a second showing, bring a contractor to get a quote on potential renovations, and carefully review disclosure documents without the fear of the home being sold out from under you. This return to a more normal, sane pace is one of the greatest advantages for buyers in the current market.

Price Reductions are Becoming a Key Feature

Another critical indicator is the frequency of price changes. In this week’s hot sheet alone, we see a notable price reduction on a property in Canyon Country, which was reduced from $1,675,000 to $1,650,000. This is a microcosm of a larger trend. Sellers who price their homes based on the peak frenzy of last year are finding that today’s more discerning buyers are not willing to overpay.

As a buyer, this means you should be paying close attention to homes that have been on the market for more than a few weeks. These are the properties where sellers may be more motivated and open to negotiation. My role as your agent is to identify these opportunities and use a data-driven approach to craft an offer that is both compelling and fair. We analyze not just the list price, but the entire market landscape, to ensure you are not paying a penny more than what a property is truly worth.

Financing Flexibility is Back

During the height of the seller’s market, cash was king, and buyers with financing were often at a disadvantage. Today, the landscape has changed. The new listings show a wide variety of acceptable financing terms, including Conventional, FHA, and VA loans. This is a clear signal that sellers are once again eager to work with financed buyers.

This is particularly great news for our veterans and first-time homebuyers. If you qualify for a VA or FHA loan, you are now on a much more level playing field. Lenders are eager to work with qualified buyers, and sellers are recognizing the value of a strong, pre-approved offer, regardless of the loan type.
