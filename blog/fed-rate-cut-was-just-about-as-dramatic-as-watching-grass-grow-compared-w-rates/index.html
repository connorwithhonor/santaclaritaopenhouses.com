Let’s cut the ribbon on the Fed’s big(ish) reveal: that September 18 trim to 4.75-5.00% is the second consecutive quarter-point dip, easing bank-to-bank borrowing like a post-shift beer after a long patrol. For context, the federal funds rate’s the economy’s thermostat – lower it, and credit cards, auto loans, everything loosens. But mortgages? They’re the rebellious teen, glued to the 10-year Treasury yield instead. At 4.2% today, yields perked up post-cut (markets sniffed it coming), keeping 30-year fixes in the 6.7-6.9% corral. Translation: on a $800K SCV median buy, you’re still staring at $5,300 monthly P&I – a measly $50 shave from pre-cut.
