Valencia Real Estate Market Update: 270 Active Listings & 53 Days to Sell in January 2026

The Valencia real estate market kicked off 2026 in a surprisingly balanced position. With 270 active listings and homes taking an average of 53 days to sell, we’re seeing a market that’s neither overheated nor stagnant—it’s what I call “professionally competitive.”

As your First Responder Realtor in Santa Clarita, I’ve analyzed the 2-year trend data to show you exactly what’s happening and what it means for sellers right now.

Valencia Market Snapshot: January 2026

  • Active Listings: 270 homes
  • Average Days to Sell: 53 days
  • Market Type: Balanced to slight seller advantage
  • Trend: Inventory down from peak, days-to-sell stabilizing

The 2-Year Valencia Trend (2024-2026)

Looking at the data from January 2024 to January 2026, here’s what stands out:

Inventory Rollercoaster

Valencia’s inventory hit some extremes over the past two years:

  • January 2024: 184 active listings (low inventory, seller’s market)
  • Peak (June 2025): 414 active listings (buyer’s market territory)
  • January 2026: 270 active listings (balanced market)

That’s a 47% increase from January 2024 to January 2026, but it’s actually 35% down from the mid-2025 peak. What does this tell us? The market absorbed the excess inventory, and we’ve settled into equilibrium.

Days to Sell: The Real Story

Here’s where it gets interesting for sellers:

  • January 2024: 31 days to sell (fast-moving market)
  • Slowest Period (Feb 2025): 73 days to sell (buyer leverage)
  • January 2026: 53 days to sell (normal market pace)

We’re seeing 71% longer market times compared to January 2024, but that’s actually good news for sellers because:

  1. Quality trumps speed - Homes that are well-prepared and priced correctly are still moving
  2. Less panic pricing - Sellers have time to market properly instead of racing to the bottom
  3. Serious buyers only - With 53 days on market, tire-kickers have moved on

What This Means for Valencia Home Sellers in 2026

1. Pricing Strategy is Critical

In January 2024, you could overprice by 5-10% and still get offers within a month. Not anymore.

With 270 homes to choose from and buyers taking 53 days to decide, your home needs to be priced within 2-3% of market value from day one. Overpricing means you’ll sit while properly-priced homes sell around you.

My approach: I use live MLS data (not Zillow estimates) and mandatory buyer feedback from every showing to position your home competitively from the start.

2. Condition Matters More Than Ever

When inventory was at 184 homes, buyers had to compromise. Now? They can be picky.

Homes that check all the boxes are selling in 30-40 days. Homes with deferred maintenance or outdated features are sitting 70+ days and eventually dropping price.

Action item: Small investments in staging, paint, and curb appeal return 200-500% when it comes to final sale price.

3. Marketing Can’t Be Generic

With 270 competing listings, your home needs to stand out. Here’s what works in 2026:

  • Professional photography (not iPhone photos)
  • Mandatory open house visitor sign-ins - I know who walked through and can follow up aggressively
  • Detailed buyer feedback - When 3 buyers say “kitchen is dated,” we know exactly what to address
  • AI-optimized listings - Your listing needs to rank on ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google SGE searches

Learn more about my accountability-based approach that includes mandatory sign-ins and professional feedback.

Valencia vs. Other SCV Cities: How Do We Compare?

Valencia’s 53 days to sell is competitive compared to other Santa Clarita Valley neighborhoods:

  • Canyon Country: 68 days to sell (slower, 225 active listings)
  • Stevenson Ranch: 68 days to sell (slower, 60 active listings)
  • Newhall: 75 days to sell (slower, 83 active listings)

Why is Valencia moving faster? Schools, master-planned communities, and proximity to major employers (Six Flags, Princess Cruises, Boston Scientific) make Valencia consistently desirable.

The Opportunity for Valencia Sellers Right Now

If you’ve been waiting for the “perfect time” to sell your Valencia home, this is it:

  1. Inventory is manageable - 270 homes isn’t overwhelming, but it’s enough to give buyers options (which keeps them engaged)
